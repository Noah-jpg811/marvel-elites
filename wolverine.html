Wolverine

Logan’s early life was marked by trauma. After a childhood trauma, Logan’s mutation activated and he fled to the wilderness where he lived for years. Over the decades, he served in multiple wars, often manipulated or used as a weapon. The Weapon X program erased much of his memory and forcibly bonded adamantium to his skeleton, turning him into a living weapon. Wolverine's story often centers on themes of identity, loss, redemption, and the constant battle between his human and animalistic sides. Though he appears rough and solitary, he often fights for the weak and has become a core member of the X-Men, forming deep bonds despite his violent nature.
